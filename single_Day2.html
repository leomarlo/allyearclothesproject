Day 1

Experimental Topology

 -  October 24, 2019

Today, I set up a little workspace for myself. It was not easy to find a building market, partly because I don’t even know the English word, partly because I couldn’t find any appropriate Georgian translation or let’s say my search engine (admittedly it’s google) couldn’t. After my Georgian classes I drove to Bricorama, which took quite some time. Yeah, it was kind of what I was looking for and I always feel happy inside these construction markets. On the list I had a large wooden board, two trestles (in German: Böcke), pins, glue strips and fabric. The wooden board I found was 16x100x2600 (in mm). Turned out to be slightly too large to fit into my Opel combi. The Georgian shop assistant suggested to glue the 10 kg wooden plate with Sellotape to the roof top.

After my dinner, which included my signature dish (1 potato, 2 carrots, 1 piece of butter and a hand-full of dill), it was time to make some first experiments.

So the low hanging fruits are t-shirts, of course. I will need some of them next year for sure. So I conducted some experimental topology. The result of these investigations were that t-shirts are topologically equivalent to the four-punctured Riemann sphere. Since I couldn’t project the shirt onto the 2 dimensional plane, I had to cut open its sides and was rewarded with these beautiful shapes. It seems I need no seams. A t-shirt can just be sewn from one piece. No patching together needed!

AllYearClothesProject

I want to create all my clothes for 2020 until the end of the year 2019. But why? And where is the starting point? My starting point is close to zero: I have no plan, no time allocation, no materials, not many contacts in the sewing world and almost no sewing resources … so far. This is not supposed to be a cooking show, where miraculously everything is prepared already. It is instead supposed to be a documented self-experiment. Starting from zero, I wonder how the project evolves and morphs into something and I wonder how I and things around me co-morph with it.
